Orientation principles encoded from Pilla et al. 2012 (PMCID: PMC3551499): use a visible intrinsically asymmetric pattern, keep the asymmetry source in a fixed corner, avoid full empty rows/lines, and prefer compact markers that remain interpretable if a few spots or peripheral rows are lost.

- For one Xenium sample area and 80 patients, 0.6 mm is usually required to keep all patients represented with 1 mm spacing. More TMAs increase patient-level replication while keeping the same per-slide constraints.
